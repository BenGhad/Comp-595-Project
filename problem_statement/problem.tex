\problemname{Dizzy Work}
\paragraph{}
For the past 20 years, Little Johnny has been working hard as a Bricklayer.
It's honest work, and he reinvests the money into his education at Hensel High School.

\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.35\textwidth]{image2}
\end{center}

\paragraph{}
The evil Mr. Ring has it out for him and has assigned Little Johnny several pages of algebra homework.
If that wasn't bad enough,
Mr. Ring was never really a fan of the real numbers, or even
the rationals: each assignment is to be solved in an integer
ring, modulus some $M$.

\paragraph{}
Johnny is no slacker, but he really needs your help with
the root-finding problems. He's been searching for answers
around the ring for hours and all he got was a spinning
headache!


\paragraph{}
Can you solve the homework and save the day?

\section*{Input}
The first line of input consists of two space-separated integers $N$ and $M$
\mbox{($1 \leq N \leq 200 ~ 000$, $2 \leq M \leq 2^{31} - 1$)}:
the number of polynomials and the modulus.
\\\\
$N$ polynomials follow, as a line which consists of
a single integer $k_i$ \mbox{($2 \leq k_i \leq 200 ~ 000$)},
followed by a line which consists of $k_i$ integers $c_{i,j}$
\mbox{($-2^{31} \leq c_{i,j} \leq 2^{31} - 1$)},
which are the coefficients of polynomial $f_i$, i.e.
\[
    f_i(x) = c_{i,1} + c_{i,2} x + c_{i,3} x^2 + \dots + c_{i,k_i} x^{k_i - 1}
\]
\\\\
You are guaranteed that the sum of all $k_i$ does not exceed $200 ~ 000$.

\section*{Output}
Print $N$ lines, each containing the smallest non-negative integer $x_i$
for which $f_i(x_i) \equiv 0 \mod M$.
\\\\
You are guaranteed that there exists such an $x_i$.
